      Hi, I’m James Pallasis, a third year Bachelor of Biomedicine student, Welcome to the South Lawn.

      My first memory of the South Lawn was when I was on my host program tour during Orientation Week.

      The tour started at the welcome gate right near South Lawn, but my host told us straight away “This is South Lawn – we are going there last, because we won’t find each other again once we get there.”

      The tour continued, and as promised, we finished at South Lawn.

      Turning from the Baillieu Library and climbing the stairs to South Lawn, I was stunned. I had never seen this many people in one place at the same time before.

      On top of that, the numbers of clubs and societies there also seemed impossible. I later found out that this wasn’t even all of them.

      At that point, I realised uni was not going to be as straightforward as high school – I had a lot more choices and was going to have to make some decisions and step out of my comfort zone – but that is how you grow as a person.

      Also, as my host predicted, I didn’t see my host group for the rest of that day – we just kind of got absorbed into the South Lawn crowd.
