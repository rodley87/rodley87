      I’ve studied quite a bit of architectural history, which definitely taught me to appreciate the beauty and history behind the architectural form of the Old Quad, which I enjoy every time I pass through from the South Lawn.

      I’ve always had quite a special relationship with the Old Quad, ever since I took the subject Foundations of Design: Representation.

      One of our assignments involved remodelling the Old Quad using a modelling software called Rhino.

      We were required to adapt this space into a stage for an interpretation of a story written by Marco Polo, then we used Photoshop to put the characters into the scenes.

      This was one of my core subjects, but it was interesting to see how different students who were studying different majors interpreted the task. Those who were majoring in graphic design were much better at using the software and those who were looking at doing landscape architecture focused more on the setting rather than the characters and story.

      This is one of the best things about the Melbourne model, as people from all backgrounds and with different end goals come together to do the same work. It really opens your eyes to different and innovative ways to approach a task, and definitely helps you learn more broadly.
